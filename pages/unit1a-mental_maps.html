A politcal Border or administrative boundary is an imaginary line that separates areas of land. Some boundaries might follow a river or other natural features but that does not automatically define one. Usually countries have multiple levels of divisions.

For example:

  • 'Level 0' – National-level
  • 'Level 1' – First sub-national level
  • 'Level 2' – Second sub-national level
  • 'Level 3' – Third sub-national level
  • ...And so on

Administrative Boundaries

We are going to concentrate our next units on the first level of each country.

To understand the administrative division of a country can bring a better understanding of statistical numbers. Statistics are often recorded in higher levels and then aggregated to a smaller levels. The representation of data in smaller levels brings the advantage of being able to see patterns. But with increasing numbers of levels there is some need of aggregation so a viewer doesn't get to confused with the amount of presented information.

Germany's level 1 – Federal states

Kenya's level 1 – Counties

South Africa's level 1 – Provinces
