Germany

Gini coefficient - Measure of income equality

The choropleth map visualizes the Gini coefficient, a measure of statistical dispersion. It is intended to represent the income inequality within a region.

Definition: The Gini coefficient measures the inequality among values of a frequency distribution. A coefficient of 0 expresses perfect equality, while a coefficient of 1 (or 100%) expresses maximal inequality between values.

This means that there is a large disparity in affluence between the poorest and wealthiest individuals in regions with a high Gini coefficient.

Definition: Indicator 1.2.2 is the proportion of men, women and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions according to national definitions.
Goal: By 2030 reduce at least by half the proportion of men, women and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions according to national definitions.

WorldBank defines those living in extreme poverty as those earning less than $1.90/day.
