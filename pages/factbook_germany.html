Factbook: Germany

Germany (German: 'Deutschland') is Europe's largest economy and second most populous state after Russia. Germany's official language is German and its capital is Berlin. As the official name 'Federal Republic of Germany' implies, the nation is a federation consisting of sixteen constituent states. Besides Germany's national constitution, each state has its own. This makes makes them mostly autonomous in regard to their internal organisation (e.g. police, education).


Geography

Location
  • Germany is situated in Central Europe, between the Baltic and the North Sea in the North and the Alps in the south. It shares borders with Denmark to the north, Poland and the Czech Republic to the east, Austria and Switzerland to the south and France, Luxembourg, Belgium and the Netherlands to the west.
Area
  • total: 357,022 sq km
  • land: 348,672 sq km
  • water: 8,350 sq km
Terrain
  • Down south is where Germany's highest point (Zugspitze, 2.963m) is situated. It is part of the Alps. Its lowest point is up north in the lowlands ('Neuendorf bei Wilster', -3.5 m). Central Germany is known for its uplands.
Climate
  • German climate is mostly temperate. While winters are usually cool and wet, summers vary from hot and dry to cool and rainy.

Education

  • Due to Germany's federal structure, education is primarily the responsibilty of each constituent state. While pre-primary education is optional, the care rates for children up to three years old is 35.5% and 91.7% for those aged three to six (2022).
    Primary education and secondary education is mandatory and apart from private schools, free of charge. German children usually go to primary school from the age of six to nine.
    After primary education, they are, in accordance with their grades, able to choose one of three school types for their secondary education.
    • A general secondary school (German: 'Hauptschule') for grades 5 through 9 that emphasizes vocational training.
    • A more practical secondary school (German: 'Realschule') for grades 5 through 10.
    • A more academic secondary school (German: 'Gymnasium') for grades 5 through 12.

    Germany's education system is not as static as it might seem. Students with good grades in lower education can always switch to the next highest one. This means, that it is possible to start in lower education and attend a university later on.
    Tertiary education is optional. Students that finished lower ('Hauptschule') or intermediate ('Realschule') education can apply for an apprenticeship. Students that graduated at a 'Gymnasium' can also attend higher education. All universities are, except for private ones, free.

Cost of Living

  • The cost of living in Germany differs between regions. Southern Germany is usually the most expensive while eastern Germany is usually the cheapest. This is especially visible when taking a look at rent. While the average rent for a one bedroom apartment in Munich's (Bayern) city centre is about €1355 (~ $1355, 2022 est.), living in the same kind of apartment in Leipzig (Sachsen) is about 60% cheaper (€550 / ~ $550, 2022 est.).
    Using the same two cities from before, one can see, that this is also the case for sustenance. A 1.5l bottle of water can be bought for €0.79 or €0.46 respectively (Munich, $0.79, 2022 est. / Leipzig, ~ $0.46, 2022 est.). The same goes for a loaf of white bread (500g) where Leipzig edges out Munich by 38 cents (€1.47 / ~ $1.47, 2022 est. / €1.85 / ~ $1.85, 2022 est.).
