Unit 4 - Correlation

Correlation is a statistical measure that expresses the extent of the linear relation of two variables or bivariate data. This means that when variables are correlated, they change together at a constant rate.

In this unit you will be able to explore the relationships between different variables of our dataset.
To start, simply drag two parameters from the left list into the right one. You can combine any two parameters except for "Population size" and "Population density".

The resulting graph is called a scatterplot. It illustrates the relationship between two variables. Variables can have positive (above-average values of variable x accompany above-average values of variable y) or negative association (above-average values of variable x accompany below-average values of variable y).
The strength of the relationship between two variables is given through the slope of the regression line.

What is linear regression

Definition: Linear regression describes a linear relationship between variables x and y of a given dataset of statistical units. Linear regression assumes that observations deviate from an underlying relationship between a dependent variable y and an independent variable x.

This relationship can be described by the linear regression line (y = ax + b) which is commonly fit to the observations by minimizing the sum of the squares of the residuals made in the results of each individual equation (Least squares approach)

The dependence between two quantities can be measured using the correlation coefficient r. It is obtained by dividing the covariance of the two variables by the product of their standard deviations.
R ranges from -1 (anti-correlation) to 0 (uncorrelated) to 1 (correlation).

When testing hypotheses, as is done in this unit, the coefficient of determination R² is important. R² is used to express the proportion of the variation in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variable. R² ranges from 0 to 1 (regression predicitions perfectly fit the data).
