Factbook: South Africa

South Africa is Africa's second largest economy and fifth most populous state. There are 11 official languages in South Africa some of which include Zulu, Xhosa, Afrikaans and English. The Republic of South Africa (Official name) is a parliamentary republic and divided into 9 provinces governed by unicameral legislatures. It is one of the few countries that has multiple capitals: they are comprised of Pretoria, Cape Town and Johannesburg.


Geography

Location
  • Africa's southernmost country lies at the tip of the continent. South Africa's coastline stretches along the South Atlantic Ocean and Indian Ocean. It shares borders with Namibia, Botswana and Zimbabwe to the north, Mozambique and Eswatini to the east and completely enclaves Lesotho.
Area
  • total: 1,219,090 sq km
  • land: 1,214,470 sq km
  • water: 4,620 sq km
Terrain
  • South Africa is made up of a vast interior plateau rimmed by rugged hills and a narrow coastal plain. Its highest point is the Mafadi mountain at a height of 3446m.
Climate
  • South Africa sports a multitude of different climatic zones. While its south-east is mostly temperate, its north-east is arid. In western South Africa, due to the Namib desert up north, there is a very arid climate. Down south there is an almost mediterannean climate with wet winters and dry, hot summers.

Education

  • South Africa's national educational system is divided into three strata.
    • General education and training: Comprised of nine compulsory years, of which six years are primary school and three years are middle school. After completing middle school, students receive a 'Basic Education and Training Certificate'.
    • Further education and training: Students choose either a vocational school or a governmental school they will attend for three more years.
    • Higher education and training: Students will receive higher education in an institution befitting their previously chosen path.
    The administrative structure of most schools does not reflect the three stratas however. Most schools are either primary schools (grades 1 to 7) or secondary schools, also known as high schools (grades 8 to 12).

Cost of living

  • The cost of living in South Africa differs from city to city. For example, the average rent for a one bedroom apartment in Bloemfontein's (Free State) city centre is about R2950 (~ $171, 2022 est.), living in the same kind of apartment in Johannesburg (Gauteng) is definitely more expensive (R6940 / ~ $402, 2022 est.).
    On the other hand, when looking at the same two cities from before, one can see, that this is not strictly the same for sustenance. While rent is cheaper in Bloemfontein, food and drinks are more expensive, i.e. a 1.5l bottle of water can be bought for R22.50 or R16.74 respectively (Bloemfontein, ~ $1.30, 2022 est. / Johannesburg, ~ $0.97, 2022 est.). This pattern continues when taking a look at a loaf of white bread (500g), where Johannesburg edges out Bloemfontein by 0.63 Rand (R15.17 / ~ $0.88, 2022 est. / R15.80 / ~ $0.92, 2022 est.) but also for example for a kilogram of white rice (R26 / ~ $1.51, 2022 est. (Johannesburg), R33 / ~ $1.91, 2022 est. (Bloemfontein)).
