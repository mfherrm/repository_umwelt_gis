Factbook: Kenya

Kenya (Swahili: Jamhuri ya Kenya) is the third-largest economy in sub-Saharan Africa and the eighth most populous state in Africa. Kenya's official languages are Swahili and English and its capital is Nairobi. The Republic of Kenya (Official name) is a presidential representative democratic republic and divided into 47 semi-autonomous counties headed by governors.


Geography

Location
  • Kenya is situated in Eastern Africa, bordering the Indian Ocean to the southeast. It shares borders with Ethopia to the north, Somalia to the east, Tanzania to the south, Uganda to the west and South Sudan to the northwest
Area
  • total: 580,367 sq km
  • land: 569,140 sq km
  • water: 11,227 sq km
Terrain
  • Kenya has a coastline on the Indian Ocean which contains swamps of mangroves. Inland are broad plains and the highlands in central Kenya. These are bisected by the Great Rift Valley. To its west there is a fertile plateau. Kenya's highest point is Mount Kenya, standing at 4985m. This is also where Kenya's permanent glaciers are located.
Climate
  • The climate of Kenya varies from tropical along the coast to more and more arid the further inland.

Education

  • Kenya's national educational system consists of three levels.
    • Eight years of compulsory primary education, beginning at age six and running through to the age of 14.
    • Four years of secondary education.
    • Four years of higher education.
    Primary and secondary education is free of charge nowadays. However, before 2008 when free secondary education was introduced, a large number of students has been unable to attend school beyond the primary level due to the cost being too high for them.
    To move on to secondary education students need to pass a national exam and obtain the "Kenyan Certificate of Primary Education". Education is highly valued in Kenya with many students taking part in after-school and weekend tutoring.

Cost of Living

  • The cost of living in Kenya differs greatly between rural and urban regions. This is especially visible when taking a look at rent. While the average rent for a one bedroom apartment in Nairobi's (Nairobi County) city centre is about KSh48770 (~ $403, 2022 est.), living in the same kind of apartment in Bungoma (Bungoma County) is about 60% cheaper (KSh15000 / ~ $124, 2022 est.).
    Using the same two cities from before, one can see, that this, for most cases, also holds true for sustenance. While water is cheaper in Nairobi, a 1.5l bottle of water can be bought for KSh89 or KSh100 respectively (Nairobi, ~ $0.74, 2022 est. / Bungoma, ~ $0.83, 2022 est.), most food items are cheaper in Bungoma. This goes for a loaf of white bread (500g) where Bungoma edges out Nairobi by 9 Kenyan Shilling (KSh53 / ~ $0.43, 2022 est. / KSh62 / ~ $0.51, 2022 est.) but also for example for a kilogram of white rice (KSh121 / ~ $1, 2022 est. (Bungoma), KSh188 / ~ $1.55, 2022 est. (Nairobi)).
